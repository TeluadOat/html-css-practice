The origin of lions

Lions originated in Africa around 2 million years ago and evolved from a common ancestor of the Panthera genus. They later migrated into Europe, the Middle East, and Asia during the Pleistocene epoch. Fossil evidence shows that lions once roamed widely across these regions. King of the Jungle

Lions, often called the "king of the jungle," symbolize strength and leadership. Though they live in savannas, not jungles, their commanding presence, majestic mane, and role as top predators earn them this royal title across cultures and legends.

Sworn Enemy

Lions' sworn enemies are often hyenas, with whom they compete fiercely for food and territory. These rivals engage in deadly clashes, driven by their overlapping diets and group dynamics, making them natural adversaries in the wild.
